# Reference List Rules (APA 7)

Normalize all references using these rules.

## Global Rules

1. Start a `References` section on its own heading.
2. Sort entries alphabetically by first author surname or group author.
3. Ensure one-to-one consistency with in-text citations.
4. Use sentence case for article/book/chapter titles.
5. Present DOI as URL form: `https://doi.org/...`.
6. Do not include retrieval dates unless content is designed to change over time.

## Core Templates

Use these templates as defaults:

1. Journal article:
`Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Journal Title, 12(3), 45-67. https://doi.org/xxxx`
2. Book:
`Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher.`
3. Chapter in edited book:
`Author, A. A. (Year). Chapter title. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), Book title (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.`
4. Webpage:
`Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL`
5. Report:
`Organization Name. (Year). Title of report (Report No. if available). Publisher. URL`
6. Thesis/dissertation:
`Author, A. A. (Year). Title of thesis [Master's thesis or Doctoral dissertation, Institution]. Database or Repository. URL`
7. Conference paper:
`Author, A. A. (Year, Month). Title of paper. In Proceedings title (pp. xx-xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/xxxx`
8. Preprint:
`Author, A. A. (Year). Title of preprint [Preprint]. Repository. URL`
9. Dataset:
`Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dataset (Version) [Data set]. Publisher. URL`
10. Software:
`Author/Organization. (Year). Software title (Version) [Computer software]. Publisher. URL`

## Incomplete Metadata Handling

1. Never fabricate missing fields.
2. Insert explicit marker for each missing critical field: `[MISSING METADATA: year]`.
3. Keep entries machine-fixable by preserving available identifiers.
4. Add a repair list in the Compliance Report for all incomplete entries.
