# Storyline and Flow

Build narrative momentum before writing slide content.

## Narrative Framework Selection

Choose one framework as primary:

1. SCQA (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer)
   - Use for executive and strategy decks.
2. Three-Act (Setup, Tension, Resolution)
   - Use for product, pitch, and persuasive talks.
3. Problem-Method-Evidence-Action
   - Use for research and technical presentations.

If the deck is mixed-purpose, choose one primary framework and map secondary elements to it.

## Required Flow Constraints

1. Define one deck-level takeaway sentence before slide drafting.
2. Ensure each slide answers: "Why is this here, now?"
3. Keep one primary message per slide.
4. Place strongest evidence before major asks.
5. End with explicit action, decision, or retention objective.

## Transition Rules

1. Start each new section with a transition sentence that links from the prior section.
2. Use cause-effect or question-answer transitions, not topic jumps.
3. Keep transitions short and forward-looking.

Transition examples:

- "Given this constraint, the next question is..."
- "To test that claim, we examined..."
- "With this result, the practical decision becomes..."

## Pacing Rules

1. Allocate 15-25% of time to context, 50-60% to core argument, and 20-30% to action/Q&A.
2. Reserve one slide-equivalent for recap in decks over 12 slides.
3. Remove slides that do not change audience understanding or decision quality.

## Common Flow Failures

1. Background overload before any clear point.
2. Results shown without framing question.
3. Recommendation presented without alternatives or evidence.
4. Final slide restates facts but gives no decision or action.
