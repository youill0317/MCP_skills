# Blind Spot Checks

Run these checks to catch missing perspectives and hidden assumptions.

## Stakeholder Blind Spots

1. Which affected group is not represented in current evidence?
2. Who bears downstream risk if the problem is misdefined?
3. Who controls a critical constraint but is not in the discussion?

## Time-Horizon Blind Spots

1. Are short-term signals masking long-term effects?
2. Is this issue seasonal, cyclical, or one-off?
3. Could intervention timing reverse interpretation?

## Context Blind Spots

1. Does the problem appear only in specific segments, regions, or channels?
2. Are edge cases being excluded without justification?
3. Are baseline conditions comparable across groups?

## Measurement Blind Spots

1. Are metrics proxying the intended construct accurately?
2. Are definitions stable across time and teams?
3. Is missing data concentrated in critical subgroups?

## Decision Blind Spots

1. Is the stated problem actually a preferred solution in disguise?
2. Is the scope too broad to be testable?
3. Is success defined independently from implementation choices?

## Tagging Rule

When a likely omission is found, mark it as `[BLIND SPOT]` and specify:

1. missing perspective/condition
2. potential impact
3. required evidence to close the gap
